# Plans

Use plans for work that spans multiple layers, requires sequencing, or has meaningful risk.

## When to create a plan

- The task affects multiple apps or packages.
- The work includes migrations, queue changes, or architectural reshaping.
- The task has several validation steps or operational risks.
- The request requires a phased rollout rather than a single-file edit.

## Plan types

- Lightweight plan
  - Good for focused changes with a small number of steps.
  - Keep it short and task-oriented.
- Execution plan
  - Good for multi-step refactors, migrations, or cross-layer changes.
  - Track progress, decisions, and validation steps as the work moves.

## What a good plan includes

- A concise goal statement.
- The main implementation phases.
- Explicit validation steps.
- Risks or operational notes when they matter.
- A small todo list that can be marked in progress and completed as work advances.

## Plan hygiene

- Keep plans close to the work and update them when scope changes.
- Prefer one coherent plan per task instead of mixing unrelated work.
- Do not let plans become stale background notes; either complete, revise, or retire them.
